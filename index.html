🔥 Phase 1 · Acute Stress
Emergency Defense Program

GO reveals activation of response to abiotic stress, ROS detoxification and hormonal signaling (ABA, JA, SA). KEGG confirms: Plant hormone signal transduction and Protein processing in ER — rapid chaperone mobilization to prevent protein misfolding. Photosynthesis, cell cycle and cytoskeleton are systematically repressed.

🛠 Phase 2 · Repair (12h)
Molecular Reconstruction Peak

GO:BP shows peak enrichment of RNA modification (p=6×10⁻⁵²), ribosome biogenesis and protein folding. KEGG: Spliceosome and Starch and sucrose metabolism activated to generate rapid energy for cellular triage. Photosynthesis and secondary metabolism remain deeply suppressed to conserve resources for repair.

🌱 Phase 3 · Adaptation (84h)
Reactivation & Stress Memory

GO:CC dominated by thylakoid (p=1.2×10⁻⁴⁰) and photosystem I/II — chloroplast structurally restored. KEGG confirms: alpha-Linolenic acid metabolism (JA precursor) and MAPK signaling provide molecular evidence of Acquired Thermotolerance. Circadian rhythm partially recovering.

The Biological Bottom Line

The plant strictly separates molecular repair from physiological growth. It first funnels all available energy into fixing broken RNA and proteins at 12h, deliberately suppressing defense and photosynthesis. Only when the internal machinery is structurally sound does the plant restart its chloroplasts and invest in long-term stress memory at 84h. Despite recovery relative to LHS, comparison with Control conditions shows that full restoration is not achieved — at 84h, the plant exhibits a partially recovered but still stress-adapted transcriptional profile, with incomplete normalization of photosynthesis and regulatory processes. Recovery is relative to stress, not a return to baseline — indicating a long-term reprogrammed state rather than full physiological restoration.

⚠ Recovery Paradox

Even at 84h, GO and KEGG show that Photosynthesis and Circadian rhythm remain significantly downregulated vs Control. The plant deliberately delays full physiological normalization to maintain a protective, energy-conservative state.

✓ Key KEGG Signature Pathways

Protein processing in ER — peaks at stress and 12h repair, subsides at 84h · Photosynthesis — suppressed throughout, reactivates only relative to stress · MAPK signaling — marker of long-term thermotolerance at 84h.

06 · Deep Dive

HSF–HSP Heat-Shock Cascade
with DREB-associated stress cross-talk

43 genes across three families — 9 DREB (AP2/ERF transcription factors), 10 HSF (heat shock factor regulators), 24 HSP (molecular chaperones) — annotated via AnnotationDbi / org.At.tair.db and profiled across LHS → LHSR12 → LHSR84.

Finding 1 · Dominant Effector Response (HSP)

All 24 HSP genes respond with massive upregulation (avg log₂FC +3.8 at LHS). Maximum activation: ATHSP22.0 at log₂FC 8.86 = 465× increase. Small HSPs (17–26 kDa) act as the primary proteostasis machinery.

Finding 2 · Recovery Paradox

HSP expression peaks at LHSR12 (12h recovery), not during stress itself. HSP26.5: 5.83 → 8.10; ATHSP22.0: 7.01 → 8.86 — the plant continues intensive protein repair after temperature returns to normal.

Finding 3 · HSF Regulatory Cascade

HSFA6A activates rapidly and transiently (3.88 → 2.67), while ATHSFA1E shows a sustained response (2.26 → 2.41). HSFB2A continues rising into recovery (1.98 → 3.47) — prolonged regulatory signaling even as temperatures normalize.

Finding 4 · DREB Stress Cross-Talk

DREB genes show divergent responses: DREB_A5 strongly activated (+3.81), DREB_A4_1 strongly repressed (−3.14). This bidirectional pattern reflects cross-talk between heat and osmotic/dehydration stress pathways — a unified abiotic stress-acclimation strategy.

The Three-Phase Adaptation Model

Biological System 🔥 Shock (LHS) 🛠 Repair (12h) 🌱 Adaptation (84h)
Photosynthesis ● Critical Halt ● Still Off ● Slow Recovery
HSP Chaperones (sHSPs) ● Strong activation ● Peak (paradox!) ● Subsiding (+2.3)
HSF Regulators ● HSFA6A / HSFA1E active ● HSFB2A peaks (+3.47) ● HSFB1/B2A memory
DREB Cross-Talk ● Mixed: A5↑ A4↓ ● DREB19 rising ● Largely off
Growth & Cell Cycle ● Arrested ● Still Stopped ● Restarting
Circadian Rhythms ● Disrupted ● Disrupted ● Recovering
Overall Conclusion

Transcriptome analysis of Arabidopsis thaliana seedlings under long heat stress (37°C, 30h) revealed a highly coordinated HSF–HSP heat-shock cascade involving 43 genes across three stress-response families. The response is dominated by small HSPs (17–26 kDa), which reached up to 465× upregulation (ATHSP22.0: log₂FC 8.86), driven by master regulators HSFA6A and ATHSFA1E. A striking recovery paradox was observed: HSP expression peaks at 12h post-stress (LHSR12), not during the stress itself, demonstrating that proteostasis maintenance continues long after temperature normalization. At 84h, ATHSP70 retains >100% of its initial response — a marker of stress memory and priming for future heat events. DREB transcription factors show bidirectional regulation, confirming significant cross-talk between the heat-stress and osmotic/dehydration signaling pathways — a unified abiotic stress-acclimation strategy in Arabidopsis thaliana.
